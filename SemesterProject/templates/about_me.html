Research

During the summer of my Junior year I obtained my first research internship at Marist College. My internship with Dr. Fitzgerald focused on the analysis of volatile organic compounds produced during the malting of barley with a portable GCMS to determine if volatile compounds can be used for the early detection of barley contaminated with Fusarium Graminearum. My Research with Dr. Fitzgerald led to my first publication in the Journal of the American Society of Brewing Chemists this year. Currently, I am working with Dr. Checchi to explore the underlying regulatory mechanisms that control chromatin dynamics. The primary focus of the Checchi group is to prevent the mutation of chromosomes that could potentially be transmitted to offspring using Caenorhabditis elegans as a model. Within the Checchi group, my project is focused on the determination of whether or not the absence of MET-2 modulates the localization of LET-418 in response to the damage of DNA.
